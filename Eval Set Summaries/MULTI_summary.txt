# HELIX CLOUD EARNINGS CALL ANALYSIS

## 1. KEY FINANCIAL METRICS

**Revenue:** $487 million (+29% YoY from $377 million in Q2 FY2024)

**EPS:** Non-GAAP net loss per diluted share of -$0.18 (improved from -$0.32 in Q2 FY2024)

**Gross Margin:** Non-GAAP gross margin of 74.1% (+230 basis points YoY from 71.8% in Q2 FY2024)

## 2. FORWARD GUIDANCE

For Q3 FY2025: Revenue expected at $510-515 million (26-27% YoY growth); non-GAAP operating loss of $15-20 million; non-GAAP net loss per share of -$0.12 to -$0.16. For full FY2025: Revenue guidance raised to $1.98-2.00 billion (up from prior $1.94-1.97 billion); non-GAAP operating loss narrowed to $70-80 million (improved from prior $75-95 million). Management expects quarterly operating income breakeven in second half of fiscal 2026.

## 3. MANAGEMENT SENTIMENT

**Mixed to Cautiously Optimistic.** Management expressed confidence in fundamental business strength, highlighting record metrics like ARR growth (34% YoY), new logo additions (214), and second consecutive quarter of positive free cash flow. However, they acknowledged competitive pressures from hyperscalers and talent market challenges, while emphasizing they're managing for long-term value rather than near-term profitability milestones.

## 4. KEY RISKS

• Intense competition from well-resourced hyperscaler native solutions (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud)
• Competitive talent market creating hiring challenges and wage pressure, particularly for senior engineers and AI researchers
• Need for continued infrastructure investments to support growth, which could impact margin progression
• Dependence on continued customer expansion and new logo acquisition to maintain growth trajectory
• Execution risk on path to profitability while balancing growth investments

## 5. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Helix Cloud delivered strong Q2 results with 29% revenue growth to $487 million and accelerating ARR growth of 34% YoY, driven by record new logo additions and healthy customer expansion. The company achieved its second consecutive quarter of positive free cash flow and raised full-year guidance while demonstrating improving operating leverage and gross margin expansion. Despite competitive pressures from hyperscalers and talent market challenges, management remains confident in their differentiated multi-cloud platform and expects to reach operating breakeven in the second half of fiscal 2026.
