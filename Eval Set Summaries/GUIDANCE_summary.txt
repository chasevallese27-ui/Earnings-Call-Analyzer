# MERIDIAN INDUSTRIAL CORPORATION Q3 FY2024 EARNINGS CALL ANALYSIS

## 1. KEY FINANCIAL METRICS

**Revenue:** $1.84 billion (up 7.0% YoY from $1.72 billion in Q3 2023)

**EPS:** $2.11 adjusted EPS, $1.87 GAAP EPS (YoY comparison not provided in transcript)

**Gross Margin:** 41.2% (up 260 basis points YoY from 38.6% in Q3 2023)

## 2. FORWARD GUIDANCE

Management explicitly stated they are not providing formal forward guidance for Q4 or full fiscal year due to uncertainty around tariff policies and trade environments. CEO Lassiter cited "rapidly evolving tariff landscape" and "meaningful uncertainty around trade policy" as making it "genuinely impossible" to provide reliable targets. Management indicated they hope to reinstate guidance next quarter and will reassess the situation at the Q4 earnings call.

## 3. MANAGEMENT SENTIMENT

**Cautious but Confident:** Management demonstrated a cautious tone regarding near-term uncertainty while maintaining confidence in underlying business fundamentals. The decision to suspend guidance reflects prudent risk management rather than operational weakness. Leadership emphasized business health, solid execution, and structural improvements while being transparent about external challenges beyond their control.

## 4. KEY RISKS

• Rapidly evolving tariff rates on steel, aluminum, and specialty materials affecting cost structure
• Trade policy uncertainty between US, EU, and Asian markets impacting demand forecasting
• Customer inventory and supply chain strategy changes due to tariff environment
• Higher logistics costs in Industrial Systems segment
• Customers holding orders in shorter booking windows, reducing backlog reliability as leading indicator
• End-market demand uncertainty in Europe and Asia affecting capital spending plans

## 5. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Meridian Industrial reported strong Q3 results with 7% revenue growth and significant gross margin expansion of 260 basis points year-over-year, driven by pricing actions, favorable raw material costs, and operational improvements. However, management suspended forward guidance citing unprecedented uncertainty from evolving tariff policies and trade environments that make reliable forecasting impossible over the next 1-3 quarters. While the underlying business fundamentals remain solid with strong cash generation and debt reduction, external trade policy volatility represents the primary near-term challenge for the company.
