# NVIDIA Q1 FY2025 Earnings Call Analysis

## 1. KEY FINANCIAL METRICS

**Revenue:**
- Q1 FY2025: $26.0 billion (up 122% YoY, up 18% sequentially)

**EPS:**
- Q1 FY2025: $6.12 (up 131% YoY vs. $2.48 in Q1 FY2024)

**Gross Margin:**
- Q1 FY2025: 78.4% (up from 64.6% in Q1 FY2024, improvement of ~1,380 basis points YoY)

## 2. FORWARD GUIDANCE

Management provided Q2 FY2025 guidance with revenue expected at $28.0 billion (plus or minus 2%). GAAP and non-GAAP gross margins are expected to be approximately 74.6% and 75.5%, respectively. The company expects some near-term margin pressure during the Blackwell ramp due to supply chain qualification and early production economics, but remains confident in long-term margin profile. Management indicated Blackwell production ramp will continue throughout the year, with the next-generation architecture generating tremendous customer interest.

## 3. MANAGEMENT SENTIMENT

**Bullish** - Management displayed exceptionally confident and optimistic sentiment throughout the call. CEO Jensen Huang described this as an "exceptional quarter" and emphasized they are in the "very early innings of what will be a multi-decade build-out of AI infrastructure." The tone was enthusiastic about demand sustainability, with Huang specifically addressing concerns about speculative ordering by asserting that demand is "real and structural" with immediate deployment by customers.

## 4. KEY RISKS

• Near-term gross margin pressure during Blackwell architecture ramp and supply chain scaling
• Complexity of new Blackwell product manufacturing and qualification processes
• Potential supply constraints continuing to exceed demand
• General forward-looking statement risks regarding AI market development timing
• Dependency on continued AI infrastructure investment by hyperscalers and enterprises

## 5. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

NVIDIA delivered exceptional Q1 FY2025 results with revenue of $26.0 billion (up 122% YoY) and EPS of $6.12 (up 131% YoY), driven primarily by unprecedented demand for AI infrastructure and their H100/H200 GPU platforms. The company's Data Center segment reached $22.6 billion in revenue (up 427% YoY) while gross margins expanded dramatically to 78.4%, reflecting strong pricing power and favorable product mix. Management expressed highly bullish sentiment about the sustainability of AI demand, emphasizing they are in the early stages of a multi-decade AI infrastructure build-out, while providing strong Q2 guidance of $28.0 billion revenue despite expecting some margin pressure during the upcoming Blackwell architecture ramp.
