CRESTWOOD BRANDS, INC.
Q4 FISCAL YEAR 2024 EARNINGS CALL TRANSCRIPT
Ticker: CRWD
February 20, 2025

OPERATOR: Good morning, and welcome to Crestwood Brands' Fourth Quarter and Full Year Fiscal 2024 Earnings Conference Call. All participants will be in a listen-only mode until the question-and-answer session. Today's call is being recorded. I would now like to turn the call over to Jennifer Lacey, Senior Director of Investor Relations. Jennifer, please go ahead.

JENNIFER LACEY: Thank you, operator, and good morning, everyone. Thank you for joining us today for Crestwood Brands' fourth quarter fiscal 2024 earnings call. With me are Gary Fielding, our President and Chief Executive Officer, and Diane Holloway, our Chief Financial Officer.

We will begin with prepared remarks from Gary and Diane, and then we will open the call for questions. I'd like to remind everyone that this call contains forward-looking statements. These statements are subject to risks and uncertainties that could cause actual results to differ materially from those anticipated. Please see the risk factor disclosures in our most recent annual report on Form 10-K and our other filings with the SEC.

I'll now turn it over to Gary.

GARY FIELDING: Thank you, Jennifer, and good morning to all of you joining us. I appreciate you taking the time to be here.

I am very pleased to report that Crestwood Brands closed fiscal 2024 with continued momentum, and I believe we are entering fiscal 2025 from a position of real strength. The fourth quarter reflected the quality and resilience of this business, and I want to take a few minutes to walk through the key themes that defined our performance.

First and foremost, we saw broad-based strength across our portfolio. Our core consumer categories performed admirably, and results were in line with our expectations heading into the quarter. The demand environment remained constructive, and we were encouraged by the continued engagement of consumers with the Crestwood family of brands. Whether you look at our home care franchise, our personal wellness lines, or our seasonal and occasion-based product families, the story is consistent: consumers continue to choose Crestwood.

We remain encouraged by the trajectory of the business. The initiatives we have been executing against — whether in brand investment, channel expansion, or consumer insight capability — are reinforcing our competitive positioning in ways we believe will compound over time. We believe we are building a more durable, more agile, and more consumer-centric version of Crestwood Brands, and the fourth quarter gave us further evidence that we are on the right path.

I also want to acknowledge the broader operating environment. It has not been without its challenges. But what I'm proud of is how our teams responded — with creativity, with discipline, and with a consistent focus on what matters most: delivering value to our consumers and our retail partners. That is the culture we are building at Crestwood, and I think the results speak to it.

We saw continued momentum in our international business as well, with meaningful progress in several priority markets that we have been investing in over the past two years. The foundation we are laying internationally is one of the things I am most excited about as I think about the years ahead.

On the innovation front, we had a productive fourth quarter of launches, and we believe our pipeline heading into fiscal 2025 is one of the strongest we have ever had. Consumer response to the new additions to our portfolio has been encouraging, and we are monitoring early performance indicators with optimism.

To sum up: we had a strong close to the fiscal year, we are entering fiscal 2025 with confidence, and we believe Crestwood Brands is well-positioned for long-term growth. The fundamentals of this business are healthy, and our team is focused and energized.

I will now turn it over to Diane to share more on the financial side.

DIANE HOLLOWAY: Thank you, Gary, and good morning, everyone.

I am pleased to share that Crestwood Brands' financial performance in the fourth quarter reflected the operational and commercial progress Gary described. We are pleased with our financial performance for the quarter and for the full fiscal year.

From a profitability standpoint, the work our teams have done on cost discipline remained a priority throughout the year, and the fourth quarter was no exception. We continued to manage our cost structure thoughtfully and with a focus on sustainable efficiency, and we saw improvement across key metrics as a result of those efforts. The investments we have made in supply chain optimization and procurement over the past several years are contributing to a more resilient financial model.

On gross margins, we saw improvement driven by favorable category mix, the benefits of our ongoing productivity programs, and the continued maturation of pricing strategies we implemented earlier in the fiscal year. We believe this positions us well as we enter the new fiscal year.

Our balance sheet is healthy. We have maintained the financial flexibility we need to continue investing in the business, pursuing bolt-on acquisition opportunities where they make strategic sense, and returning capital to shareholders in a disciplined manner. We are pleased with our cash position and with the quality of our working capital management during the quarter.

Operating expenses were managed with discipline. We continued to prioritize investments in consumer-facing brand building and in the capabilities needed to support our long-term growth agenda, while maintaining rigor around back-office and overhead costs. We believe the operating expense structure we have built is appropriate for a business at our stage and scale.

Looking ahead, we remain optimistic about the year ahead. We are well-positioned for long-term growth, and we believe the actions we have taken to strengthen the business over the past several years set us up to deliver value for shareholders. We are not providing specific financial targets for fiscal 2025, as we prefer to let our performance speak for itself as the year unfolds. We remain confident in the direction of the business and look forward to updating you on our progress each quarter.

I'll now turn it back to Jennifer.

JENNIFER LACEY: Thank you, Diane. Operator, please open the line for analyst questions.

On margins specifically, the improvement I'd point to is structural rather than episodic — it's the result of programs we have been running for multiple quarters, and we believe it is sustainable.

PAUL TREMBLAY (Jefferies): Okay. And on the guidance side — you mentioned you're not providing specific targets. I just want to understand: is there any framework you can give us? Organic growth, margin expansion, anything? Even rough directional color?

DIANE HOLLOWAY: Paul, we appreciate the question, and we understand the desire for more structure. We remain optimistic about the year ahead, and we believe the underlying demand environment for our categories is constructive. On organic growth, we're focused on executing against our innovation pipeline, deepening our retail partnerships, and continuing to expand internationally — those are the growth drivers. On margins, cost discipline will remain a priority, and we believe we have the opportunity to continue improving our profitability profile. But we are not going to put specific numbers around those expectations today. We are well-positioned for long-term growth, and we believe the best way to demonstrate that is through our results over time.
