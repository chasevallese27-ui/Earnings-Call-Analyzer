# Reading International, Inc. (RDI) Q4 2025 Earnings Call Analysis

## 1. KEY FINANCIAL METRICS

**Revenue:**
- Q4 2025: $50.3 million (decreased $8.3 million quarter-over-quarter)
- Full Year 2025: $203 million (decreased 4% year-over-year)

**EPS:**
- Q4 2025: Loss of $0.11 per share (vs. loss of $0.10 in Q4 2024)
- Full Year 2025: Loss of $0.62 per share (improved from loss of $1.58 in 2024)

**Gross Margin:**
- Not Disclosed

## 2. FORWARD GUIDANCE

Management expects 2026 to be a significantly stronger year, citing an improved movie slate with major releases including The Devil Wears Prada 2, Toy Story 5, Supergirl, Minions 3, and Avengers: Doomsday. Global cinemas are already trading ahead by over 11% on a U.S. dollar basis for the period from January 1 through April 1, 2026. The company believes 2026 will be the best post-pandemic box office year to date. Management plans to complete asset sales (Cinema 1, 2 & 3 building, Newbury Yard property, and Napier property) by the end of Q3 2026 to address liquidity needs and debt maturities.

## 3. MANAGEMENT SENTIMENT

**Mixed to Cautiously Optimistic** - While management expressed disappointment with Q4 2025 results due to a weaker film slate compared to the strong Q4 2024 performance, they remain confident about the company's strategic positioning. The tone reflects resilience, highlighting successful debt reduction of nearly 10%, continued positive EBITDA for six straight quarters, and record-setting food & beverage performance. Management emphasized their ability to navigate challenges without government assistance or shareholder dilution.

## 4. KEY RISKS

• Multiple debt facilities maturing in 2026 requiring refinancing or asset monetization
• Continued attendance levels below pre-pandemic levels across cinema operations
• Foreign exchange headwinds from weakening Australian and New Zealand currencies
• Ongoing negotiations with landlords for occupancy cost reductions
• Potential for additional cinema closures over the next 12-18 months
• Dependence on asset sales to meet liquidity needs and debt obligations
• Legal uncertainty surrounding the Reading Viaduct property value

## 5. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Reading International reported disappointing Q4 2025 results with revenue declining to $50.3 million due to a weaker film slate compared to the exceptional Q4 2024 performance, though the company achieved six consecutive quarters of positive EBITDA and record food & beverage performance. Management successfully reduced global debt by nearly 10% to $185.1 million through strategic asset sales while maintaining operational discipline, resulting in improved full-year operating performance despite revenue headwinds. The company is positioned for a stronger 2026 with an improved film slate already showing 11% revenue growth year-to-date, though near-term focus remains on addressing multiple debt maturities through planned asset monetization including the Cinema 1, 2 & 3 building sale.
