# NVIDIA Q1 FY2025 Earnings Call Analysis

## 1. KEY FINANCIAL METRICS

**Revenue:**
- Q1 FY2025: $26.0 billion (up 122% YoY, up 18% sequentially)
- Exceeded guidance of $24.0 billion

**EPS:**
- Q1 FY2025: $6.12 diluted EPS (up 131% YoY)
- Q1 FY2024: $2.48 diluted EPS

**Gross Margin:**
- Q1 FY2025: 78.4% (up from 64.6% in Q1 FY2024)
- Improvement of approximately 1,380 basis points YoY

## 2. FORWARD GUIDANCE

For Q2 FY2025:
- Revenue expected at $28.0 billion, plus or minus 2%
- GAAP gross margin expected at approximately 74.6%
- Non-GAAP gross margin expected at approximately 75.5%
- GAAP operating expenses expected at approximately $4.0 billion
- Non-GAAP operating expenses expected at approximately $2.8 billion
- GAAP and non-GAAP tax rates expected at 13.5% and 15.0%, respectively

Management indicated Blackwell architecture is on track with production ramp throughout the year, though early ramp economics will pressure margins initially before improving at scale.

## 3. MANAGEMENT SENTIMENT

**Bullish** - Management displayed highly optimistic sentiment throughout the call. Jensen Huang characterized demand as "never stronger" and emphasized being in the "very early innings" of a multi-decade AI infrastructure build-out. The tone was confident and expansive, with management highlighting structural demand drivers, strong customer relationships, and technological leadership. They expressed conviction about market positioning and long-term growth prospects while acknowledging they are at the center of global AI transformation.

## 4. KEY RISKS

• Supply chain complexity and margin pressure during Blackwell product ramp
• Potential customer double-ordering or over-ordering concerns amid supply constraints
• Manufacturing scaling challenges with new, more complex Blackwell architecture
• Early production economics unfavorability during new product transitions
• Dependency on continued AI infrastructure investment from cloud providers and enterprises

## 5. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

NVIDIA delivered exceptional Q1 results with revenue of $26.0 billion (up 122% YoY) and gross margins of 78.4%, driven by unprecedented demand for AI infrastructure and their dominant H100/H200 GPU platforms. Management expressed high confidence in structural demand drivers and positioned the company at the center of a multi-decade AI transformation, with strong visibility into future quarters and no signs of speculative ordering. While the upcoming Blackwell architecture ramp may create near-term margin pressure, the company maintains a bullish outlook on long-term growth prospects and technological leadership in the AI ecosystem.
