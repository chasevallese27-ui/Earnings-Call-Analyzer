# ROOTS Q4 2025 EARNINGS CALL ANALYSIS

## 1. KEY FINANCIAL METRICS

**Revenue:**
- Q4 2025: CAD 115.5 million (up 4.2% YoY from CAD 110.8 million)
- Full Year 2025: CAD 277.7 million (up 5.6% YoY from CAD 262.9 million)

**EPS:**
- Q4 2025: CAD 0.37 per share (vs. net loss of CAD 0.54 per share in Q4 2024)
- Full Year 2025: CAD 0.12 per share (vs. net loss of CAD 0.83 per share in fiscal 2024)

**Gross Margin:**
- Q4 2025: 61.8% (up 50 basis points YoY)
- Full Year 2025: 61.3% (up 150 basis points YoY, achieving record high)

## 2. FORWARD GUIDANCE

Management expects to reduce overall marketing spend in fiscal 2026 while maintaining efficiency and focusing on areas with the best return on investment. The company is monitoring macro and trade environment developments closely and plans to mitigate their impact while continuing to invest in long-term brand growth. Management emphasized maintaining their consistent strategy of strengthening core franchises, expanding into complementary categories, and enhancing omni-channel experience. No specific numerical guidance was provided for fiscal 2026.

## 3. MANAGEMENT SENTIMENT

**Mixed to Cautiously Optimistic.** Management expressed pride in achieving record gross margins, strong cash flow generation, and meaningful earnings improvement while highlighting successful product reception and operational improvements. However, they acknowledged operating in a "dynamic environment" and expressed caution about macro and trade headwinds. The tone was balanced between celebrating achievements and maintaining vigilance about external challenges.

## 4. KEY RISKS

• Dynamic consumer environment and macro headwinds affecting retail demand
• Potential freight cost increases due to elevated oil prices
• Impact of evolving trade environment and policy changes
• U.S. duties on e-commerce sales following elimination of duty-free de minimis exemption
• Foreign exchange impacts on USD inventory purchases
• Complexity in adapting to AI-powered customer product discovery platforms
• Distribution center transition costs and operational risks during Metro Supply Chain partnership implementation

## 5. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Roots delivered strong Q4 2025 results with revenue growing 4.2% to CAD 115.5 million and achieving a record gross margin of 61.3% for the full year, while significantly improving profitability from a net loss to CAD 0.12 per share. The company demonstrated operational excellence through successful product launches like Cloud Fleece, effective marketing campaigns including the Seth Rogen collaboration, and strategic initiatives such as AI-driven inventory management and the new distribution partnership with Metro Supply Chain. Despite acknowledging macro challenges, management remains focused on disciplined execution of their strategy while the board of directors conducts a strategic review to maximize shareholder value, though no specific transaction is guaranteed.
