NVIDIA CORPORATION
Q1 FISCAL YEAR 2025 EARNINGS CALL TRANSCRIPT
May 22, 2024

OPERATOR: Good afternoon, and welcome to NVIDIA's First Quarter Fiscal Year 2025 Earnings Conference Call. All participants will be in a listen-only mode until the question-and-answer session. Today's call is being recorded. I would now like to turn the call over to Simona Jankowski, Vice President of Investor Relations. Please go ahead.

SIMONA JANKOWSKI: Thank you. Good afternoon, everyone, and welcome to NVIDIA's First Quarter Fiscal Year 2025 Earnings Call. With me today are Jensen Huang, President and Chief Executive Officer, and Colette Kress, Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer.

I'd like to remind you that today's discussion contains forward-looking statements based on management's current expectations. These statements are subject to risks and uncertainties that could cause actual results to differ materially. We encourage you to review NVIDIA's filings with the SEC for a discussion of those risks. Our non-GAAP financial results exclude stock-based compensation and other adjustments. Reconciliations of GAAP to non-GAAP measures are included in our earnings release.

I will now turn the call over to Jensen.

JENSEN HUANG: Thank you, Simona, and good afternoon to everyone joining us today.

We have had an exceptional quarter. The age of AI is here, and NVIDIA sits at the center of it. Demand for our products has never been stronger, and I believe we are still in the very early innings of what will be a multi-decade build-out of AI infrastructure across the globe.

Let me start with what is driving this moment. Every major cloud service provider, every enterprise IT organization, every government, and virtually every industry on the planet is racing to build AI capability. And at the foundation of all of that AI — the training, the inference, the reasoning — is NVIDIA compute. We are the engine of this transformation.

Our data center business had a historic quarter. H100 demand continues to far exceed supply, and I am pleased to report that our next-generation Blackwell architecture is on track and generating tremendous interest from customers. Blackwell will take everything we have built with Hopper and multiply it many times over. We are confident in our ability to ramp production throughout the year.

Beyond the hardware, NVIDIA's software platform, CUDA, continues to deepen its moat. Millions of developers write code on CUDA. Our networking business, including InfiniBand and Ethernet, is scaling rapidly as customers build out massive GPU clusters that require the fastest interconnects available. The full-stack nature of what we offer — from silicon to systems to software to developer tools — is something no competitor can replicate overnight.

Automotive is also emerging as a meaningful growth driver. Our DRIVE platform is being designed into next-generation vehicles by nearly every major automaker. And our inference business is growing as models move from research into production.

Simply put: the world is building a new kind of infrastructure. AI factories. And NVIDIA is the company supplying what those factories run on.

I'll now turn it over to Colette to walk through the financials.

COLETTE KRESS: Thank you, Jensen, and good afternoon, everyone.

I'll walk through our Q1 fiscal 2025 results on a non-GAAP basis unless otherwise noted.

Starting with the top line: Q1 revenue was $26.0 billion, up 122% year over year and up 18% sequentially. This result exceeded our guidance of $24.0 billion and reflects the extraordinary demand environment for AI infrastructure.

By segment: Data Center revenue was $22.6 billion, up 427% year over year. This growth was driven by our Hopper GPU architecture, which continues to ramp across cloud providers, consumer internet companies, and enterprise customers. The H100 and H200 platforms remained our primary revenue drivers in the quarter. Gaming revenue was $2.6 billion, up 18% year over year, as the PC gaming market continues its recovery. Professional Visualization revenue was $427 million, up 45% year over year. Automotive revenue was $329 million, up 11% year over year.

Gross margin for the quarter was 78.4%, up from 64.6% in Q1 of fiscal 2024 — an improvement of approximately 1,380 basis points year over year. This reflects strong pricing power, favorable product mix toward our higher-margin data center products, and improving operational efficiencies in our supply chain.

Operating expenses were $1.3 billion. Operating income was $16.9 billion, representing an operating margin of 65%. Net income was $14.9 billion.

Earnings per diluted share were $6.12, up 131% year over year, compared to $2.48 in Q1 fiscal 2024.

We generated $15.4 billion of cash flow from operations in the quarter. We returned $7.8 billion to shareholders through share repurchases and dividends. We ended the quarter with $31.4 billion in cash, cash equivalents, and marketable securities.

Turning to guidance for Q2 fiscal year 2025: We expect revenue of $28.0 billion, plus or minus 2%. We expect GAAP and non-GAAP gross margins of approximately 74.6% and 75.5%, respectively. GAAP operating expenses are expected to be approximately $4.0 billion. Non-GAAP operating expenses are expected to be approximately $2.8 billion. GAAP and non-GAAP tax rates are expected to be 13.5% and 15.0%, respectively.

I'll now turn it back to Simona for Q&A.

SIMONA JANKOWSKI: Thank you, Colette. We will now open the call for questions. Operator, please go ahead.

OPERATOR: Thank you. Our first question comes from Stacy Rasgon of Bernstein Research. Please go ahead.

STACY RASGON (Bernstein Research): Hi, guys. Thanks for taking my question. Jensen, the data center number was absolutely remarkable. I guess my question is around sustainability. Can you talk about the visibility you have into demand? And is there any concern that customers are double-ordering or over-ordering in anticipation of supply constraints easing?

JENSEN HUANG: Stacy, great question, and one I take seriously. The honest answer is that the demand we are seeing is real and structural. This is not inventory stocking. Cloud providers are deploying immediately — they are capacity-constrained and every chip we ship goes into production systems right away. When I talk to CEOs of hyperscalers and enterprises, the conversation is always the same: they need more, they need it faster, and they are not slowing down their plans. The workloads are real. The models are real. The revenue these companies are generating from AI services is real. We have strong backlog and visibility into the next several quarters, and the pipeline of new customer engagements continues to grow. I am not seeing speculative ordering. I am seeing urgent deployment.

OPERATOR: Thank you. Our next question comes from C.J. Muse of Cantor Fitzgerald. Please go ahead.

C.J. MUSE (Cantor Fitzgerald): Thank you. Colette, I wanted to ask about margins. You came in well above expectations on gross margin this quarter. The Q2 guide for gross margin steps down somewhat. Can you help us understand the puts and takes? And how should we think about margins as Blackwell ramps?

COLETTE KRESS: C.J., thank you for that question. The Q1 gross margin of 78.4% was a strong result, driven by continued mix shift toward data center, favorable pricing on our H100 and H200 products, and some supply chain favorability. As we move into Q2 and into the Blackwell ramp, we will see some near-term pressure on margins as we qualify and scale new supply chains, bring new manufacturing partners online, and move through the early ramp curve. Blackwell is a more complex product and early ramp economics are always less favorable than mature production. That said, we expect Blackwell gross margins to be very healthy at scale, and we remain confident in our long-term gross margin profile. We will provide more color on that as the ramp progresses through the year.

SIMONA JANKOWSKI: Thank you all for joining us today. We look forward to speaking with you again next quarter. Have a great evening.

OPERATOR: Thank you. This concludes NVIDIA's First Quarter Fiscal Year 2025 Earnings Conference Call. You may now disconnect.
